# The Two-Step Voice Pipeline

<Info>
  Source: [https://x.com/knoxtwts/status/2023749545939624246](https://x.com/knoxtwts/status/2023749545939624246)
</Info>

## The problem with raw AI voice

Even ElevenLabs sounds too clean. Too professional. Like a studio recording.

When your video looks like someone filming in their bedroom, studio-quality audio creates a mismatch your brain catches immediately — even if you can't explain why. Something just feels off.

## The fix

<Steps>
  <Step title="CapCut voice normalization">
    Take your script audio and run it through CapCut's voice change feature. Pick any random voice from their library — it doesn't matter which one.

    What this does: normalizes everything. Strips weird artifacts, removes inconsistencies, kills any robotic quality. Gives you a clean foundation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="ElevenLabs voice application">
    Take that normalized audio into ElevenLabs. Now apply the actual voice you want.

    Result: sounds like a real person in a real room, because you built the voice on a clean consistent base.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Additional tips

- **Record your own voice as the source** (speech-to-speech) — real human speech patterns are the best foundation
- **Clone that voice in ElevenLabs** for consistency across all your videos
- **Run everything through Adobe Podcast** — cleans artifacts that stick out once layered over video
- **Add background room tone in CapCut**: coffee shop ambience, light street noise, TV in background

## Punctuation as vocal direction

How you punctuate your script controls how the voice sounds:

| Punctuation | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| Period `.` | Pause |
| Comma `,` | Breath |
| Ellipsis `...` | Trailing thought |
| Em dash `—` | Abrupt cut |
