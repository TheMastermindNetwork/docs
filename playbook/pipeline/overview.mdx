# The Full Pipeline

AI UGC (User Generated Content) means creating videos that look like real people genuinely talking about a product — made entirely with AI tools. No camera, no actors, no studio. Just your laptop and a few tools.

## The sequence

```
Script → Character → Voice → Lip Sync → Edit
```

Every video you make follows this exact sequence. Learn each step once and you can produce 30+ videos in a day.

## The steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Script">
    Write your script in Claude. Batch 10+ variations in one session. Time it with a stopwatch — your script duration must exactly match your video generation duration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Character">
    Generate your character in Nano Banana Pro using JSON prompting, not keywords. The JSON method is what separates realistic output from obvious AI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Voice">
    Run your script through CapCut's voice change first to normalize, then apply your cloned ElevenLabs voice. This two-step process is what makes AI voices sound real.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Lip Sync">
    Feed your character image and voice audio into WAN LipSync (long-form) or Kling (short clips). The model animates the face to match the audio.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit">
    Assemble in CapCut: add film grain, background noise, captions. Export 1080p. Run through Telegram compression.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Full timeline

**25–35 minutes per finished video** once your templates are set up.
