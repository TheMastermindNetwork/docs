# Master Checklist

Do this every single time. No exceptions.

<Info>
  Source: [https://x.com/knoxtwts/status/2029917141776859297](https://x.com/knoxtwts/status/2029917141776859297)
</Info>

## Before you start

- Use Claude for scripts, not ChatGPT. Claude produces copy that sounds like a real human wrote it. ChatGPT defaults to flat corporate tone.
- Write every prompt like a creative brief — who exactly is this for, what's the deliverable, examples of tone you want, words to never use.
- Build a system prompt before any session. Example: *"You write supplement ads for men 30–50. Short sentences. Never use exclamation marks. Always lead with the pain point."*

## Character & image

- Generate in Nano Banana Pro using detailed JSON prompts — not keywords
- Always include: *"visible pores, natural oiliness, subtle imperfections"* and *"no smoothing, no airbrushing, no beauty filters"*
- Always add to avoid list: *"CGI, AI-generated look, smooth plastic skin, studio lighting, perfect symmetry"*
- Generate reference images across multiple angles before you need them

## Scripting

- Write 5–10 variations in one Claude session (30 mins). You need volume to find winners.
- Add small complaints to product reviews. Perfect reviews sound fake.
- Add filler words like *"euuhh..."* and natural pauses. Lip sync sounds dramatically more human.
- Throw in a self-correction or casual tangent. Real people don't speak in perfect sentences.

## Video generation

- Direct scenes like a film director: *"medium shot, woman picks up serum bottle, examines label, looks at camera with slight skepticism, handheld feel, soft window light from left, shallow depth of field"*
- Use cinematic motion verbs: dolly push, whip-pan, crash zoom, rack focus, tracking shot
- Always include *"NO over-reaction"* and *"natural blinking"* in every prompt
- Budget 3–10 generations per usable clip. Same prompt produces different results every run.
- Kling 3.0: max 2 prompts per generation or quality degrades
- Kling 3.0: script timing must **exactly** match your generation duration — read it out loud and time it with a stopwatch

## Voice & audio

- Clone one voice in ElevenLabs and use it across everything for consistency
- Run voiceover through Adobe Podcast before final assembly — cleans artifacts
- Punctuation IS vocal direction: periods = pauses, commas = breaths, `...` = trailing thought

## Editing

- Add 2–3% film grain in CapCut — removes the clean digital look instantly
- Drop a light background noise layer under voiceover (coffee shop, room tone, street)
- Export at 1080p not 4K — lower res blends better with real UGC in the feed
- Run through one round of compression: upload to Telegram, download, reupload
- Cut the first 3 seconds faster than your instinct says
