# Prompting Video Models Like a Director

## The fundamental mistake

Describing a scene instead of directing one.

<CodeGroup>
  ```text Wrong
  "A woman uses skincare in her bathroom"
  ```

  ```text Right
  "Medium shot, woman picks up amber glass serum bottle from white marble counter,
  examines label, turns to face camera with slight smile, soft morning light from
  left window, shallow depth of field, slight handheld camera sway"
  ```
</CodeGroup>

The wrong version is narrative. The model doesn't know what to do with it. The right version is direction.

## What every prompt needs

- **Shot type:** medium shot, close-up, wide shot
- **Subject action:** exactly what the person does
- **Camera behavior:** handheld sway, static, dolly push, rack focus
- **Lighting:** where it comes from, color temperature
- **Depth of field:** shallow (blurry background) or deep (everything in focus)

## Always include these

```
"NO over-reaction"        // without this, characters overact constantly
"natural blinking"        // without this, characters stare like they're broken
"slight handheld micro-jitter"  // makes it feel like a real phone video
```

## Cinematic motion verbs

Use these to get natural movement:

`dolly push` · `whip-pan` · `crash zoom` · `rack focus` · `tracking shot` · `slow cinematic push-in` · `shallow depth of field` · `micro-parallax`
