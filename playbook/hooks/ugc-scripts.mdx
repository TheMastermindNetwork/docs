# UGC Scripts That Sound Human

## The biggest mistake beginners make

Writing scripts that are too clean, too structured, too perfect.

Real UGC has:
- Genuine hesitation: *"I was honestly kind of skeptical at first..."*
- Small complaints: *"The packaging is kind of ugly but the actual results were insane"*
- Self-corrections mid-sentence
- Casual tangents that go nowhere
- Specific details that feel lived-in, not generic

## The 4-part script structure

| Part | Timing | Goal |
|------|--------|------|
| **Hook** | 0–3s | Name the pain or create curiosity immediately |
| **Build** | 3–12s | Relate to the problem in emotional terms. Show you understand their situation. |
| **Proof** | 12–22s | Introduce the product as the solution. Show the result. Be specific. |
| **CTA** | 22–30s | Natural recommendation. "Link in bio" — not aggressive sales language. |

## How to write in batches

Give Claude the product details, the target customer description, and 2–3 competitor transcripts as reference. Ask for 10 variations at once. Run 3–5 rounds of refinement.

This takes 30 minutes and gives you a week of content to test.

## Prompt template

```
Product: [description]
Target customer: [age, gender, situation, specific pain points]
Tone: casual, conversational, like someone filming on their phone
Format: 4-part structure (hook → build → proof → CTA)
Length: 25–30 seconds when read out loud
Reference transcripts: [paste 2–3 competitor scripts]

Write 10 variations. Make each hook different. Vary the emotional angle.
Add natural imperfections — filler words, small hesitations, one small complaint.
```
