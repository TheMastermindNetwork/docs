# Hook Patterns That Actually Convert

<Info>
  Source: [https://x.com/recap_david/status/2029202419872350417](https://x.com/recap_david/status/2029202419872350417)
</Info>

The hook is the first 3 seconds of your video. It's the only thing standing between your viewer scrolling past or stopping to watch.

## The 5 hook types that consistently perform

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. POV opener">
    **Example:** *"POV: You discover something that replaces your entire morning routine"*

    Works because it immediately puts the viewer inside the scenario. They see themselves.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="2. Price contrast">
    **Example:** *"I found a $19 version of the $400 product everyone is buying"*

    Works because it triggers the deal-hunting instinct. The brain immediately wants to know what it is.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="3. Time-bound result">
    **Example:** *"My skin after 7 days of this $40 mask vs $300 treatments"*

    Works because it makes the result feel concrete and achievable in a specific timeframe.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="4. Authority cite">
    **Example:** *"My dermatologist told me this is the only serum I actually need"*

    Works because it borrows credibility from an expert while staying conversational.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="5. Replacement story">
    **Example:** *"I replaced my entire skincare routine with one thing"*

    Works because simplification is universally appealing. Everyone wants to do less.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The rule for all hooks

Be specific enough that the target viewer thinks *"this was made for me"* — not *"this could apply to anyone."*

Vague hooks generate passive scrolling. Specific hooks stop it.
