# Yaps vs UGC — Understanding the Difference

<Info>
  Source: [https://x.com/heyitsalexP/status/2025970582689337720](https://x.com/heyitsalexP/status/2025970582689337720)
</Info>

## The difference

**Traditional UGC:** Looks like an ad. Good lighting, studied composition, scripted delivery. Viewers under 30 recognize it immediately as paid content.

**A yap:** Looks like someone filmed themselves casually on an iPhone in bad lighting. Unedited. Real background noise. Slightly messy framing. Sounds like a real person talking to a friend.

## Why yaps convert better with cold audiences

Traditional UGC requires the viewer to make an instant judgment about whether they trust or like the person on screen. Many people scroll before the first word.

A yap opens with a text hook or direct statement — the viewer evaluates the content on its own merits, not the person's appearance.

## What this means for AI UGC

Your character doesn't need to look perfect.

- Slightly unflattering lighting
- Messy hair
- Basic apartment background

These make the content feel **more authentic**, not less professional. The *"looks real"* goal is more important than the *"looks good"* goal.

<Tip>
  The best-performing AI UGC often looks like it was filmed in 2 minutes on a bad day. That's the goal.
</Tip>
